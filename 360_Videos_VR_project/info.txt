Mini Project (60 marks, 30% weightage)
You will first participate in human behavioral experiments (at least 1.5-2 hrs
worth of experiments). Then, by Feb 10, you will be provided with
anonymized data from one of the experiments you took part in along with
some sample questions (non-exhaustive, you are supposed to come up with
more questions on your own) you can answer by applying statistical
methods on those datasets. Note that machine learning methods will not be
accepted. You have to primarily apply the methods taught in class with a few
additional methods if needed. However, using chatGPT to come up with
sophisticated analyses that no one understands or if you are unable to
properly interpret the results of your analyses when asked (again indicating
that you copy-pasted the output of AI without making an effort to really
understand it) will attract a big 50% penalty. Therefore, we reserve the right
to give a viva to any person/team that engages in such extreme genAI
assistance with very little understanding of the statistical methods, their
assumptions, their applicability to your data, and the appropriate
interpretation of the results.
The graded components are:
1. Experiment participation – 10 marks (experiments will have vigilence
checks, so you will lose 10 marks if you do not pay attention and do the
task to the best of your ability).
2. Report 1 – presenting visualization of variables in the data, descriptive
statistics, and any other preliminary analysis results (both descriptive and
inferential – with plots and statistics) - 10 marks.
3. Presentation of report 1 – randomly selected teams (1 per
experiment/dataset) who will receive a 1% bonus.
4. Report 2 – update of the initial report with additional inferential statistical
analyses using advanced methods such as ANOVA, regression, and GLM –
20 marks.
5. Final poster presentation (instead of class presentations); all teams - 10
marks.
Report 1 (~5-6 pages, single-spaced, font size 12) to be submitted by 10
pm Feb 21, 2025. You will forfeit the 10 marks associated with report
1 if you miss this deadline.
Report 2 (~10-12 pages, single-spaced, font size 12) to be submitted by
10 pm April 20, 2025. You will forfeit the 20 marks associated with
report 2 if you miss this deadline.
Both reports will be graded according to the same scheme (but half
the marks for report 1):
1. Introduction and explanation of the dataset, some background about
the domain (you should do some research to understand the domain)
– 2 marks
2. Methods (the analysis you did must be clear from the methods section
– this is where you describe HOW you analyzed the data) – 6 marks
3. Results (make good figures, use statistics to make your points about
what you present in the figures – this is where you describe WHAT you
found by doing the analysis) – 8 marks
4. Conclusion from the analyses above and plans for the next
steps/future directions – 2 marks
5. Formatting, citations, references – 2 marks
Total 20 marks (10 for report 1)
